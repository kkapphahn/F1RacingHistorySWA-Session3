Historic Timeline

Key Milestones That Shaped Formula 1

1950

The Beginning

The first F1 World Championship race took place at Silverstone, UK. Giuseppe Farina became the inaugural World Champion, marking the start of motorsport's premier competition.

1976

Hunt vs Lauda

The legendary rivalry between James Hunt and Niki Lauda captivated the world. Lauda's dramatic crash at the N√ºrburgring and Hunt's championship victory became F1 folklore.

1988

Senna vs Prost

McLaren dominated with Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost, winning 15 of 16 races. Their intense rivalry defined an era and produced some of F1's most memorable battles.

2000-2004

Schumacher's Dominance

Michael Schumacher and Ferrari created an unprecedented dynasty, winning five consecutive championships and rewriting the record books with 72 career victories.

2014-2020

Hybrid Era Begins

Mercedes revolutionized F1 with hybrid power units, dominating the constructors' championship. Lewis Hamilton emerged as the era's defining champion with record-breaking achievements.

2021

Historic Title Battle

The most controversial season finale in F1 history saw Max Verstappen overtake Hamilton on the last lap in Abu Dhabi, ending Mercedes' eight-year dominance in a dramatic fashion.

2022-Present

Ground Effect Revolution

New technical regulations brought ground effect aerodynamics back to F1, reshaping car designs and competitive balance. Red Bull Racing and Max Verstappen established new dominance.
